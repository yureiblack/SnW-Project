STENCIL

Stencil street art emerged in the mid-20th century as a powerful form of urban artistic and political expression, with roots in political activism and graphic design.

Pioneered by artists like Blek le Rat in Paris during the late 1970s and early 1980s, the technique involves creating repeated images by cutting designs into a template and quickly applying them to public surfaces.

By the 1990s and 2000s, artists like Banksy brought stencil art into mainstream consciousness, transforming city walls into canvases that blend social commentary, political protest, and graphic storytelling.

Today, what was once considered vandalism is now recognized as a legitimate and influential art form that continues to challenge perspectives and beautify urban landscapes.
